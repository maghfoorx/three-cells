## The Problem with "Everything Apps"

Tools like Notion are incredible. They are flexible, powerful, and customizable. But when it comes to habit tracking, **flexibility is often the enemy of consistency.**

When you use a general-purpose tool for habits, you introduce friction:
1.  **Loading Time:** Opening a large database takes time.
2.  **Decision Fatigue:** "Should I log this as a checkbox or a relation?"
3.  **Maintenance:** Templates break. Formulas need updating.

Every micro-second of friction gives your brain an excuse to skip the habit.

> "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." — James Clear, *Atomic Habits*

If your system requires you to be a database administrator just to log a glass of water, your system is broken.

## The Anatomy of a Habit

To understand why simple tools work better, we need to look at how habits are actually formed in the brain. The "Habit Loop" consists of three parts:

1.  **The Cue:** The trigger that initiates the behavior.
2.  **The Routine:** The behavior itself.
3.  **The Reward:** The benefit you get from the behavior.

**Three Cells** was built to optimize every stage of this loop, specifically for people who are tired of over-engineered systems.

## The 3-Step System to Consistency

You don't need a "Life OS." You need a simple, repeatable workflow. Here is the exact system that helped me go from sporadic bursts of motivation to 500+ day streaks:

### Step 1: The Morning Review (Journal)
Start your day by looking backward. Three Cells asks you three simple questions about yesterday.
*   What went well?
*   What could be better?
*   How did you feel? (Rated on a scale)

This takes 60 seconds but provides massive insight. You start to see patterns: *I feel terrible every time I skip breakfast.*

### Step 2: The Daily Action (Habits)
Pick 3-5 core habits. Not 20. Just the ones that actually move the needle.
*   Workout
*   Read
*   Deep Work

Track them with a single tap. If you miss one, don't panic. Just try to keep the heatmap green.

### Step 3: The Weekly Audit (Metrics)
Once a week, look at your graphs. Three Cells automatically turns your manual inputs into beautiful charts.
*   Are you sleeping more?
*   Is your mood trending up or down?
*   Are you sticking to your reading habit?

No formulas required. The data is just *there*.
